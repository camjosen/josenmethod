From the project-level README:

> "The `evidence` module for storing, organizing, and retrieving evidence of a human's progress along `paths`, as well as their "knowledge state" more broadly. Most evidence is produced by `guides` using data from human interaction with `tools` during `sessions`. Evidence is useful for personalized learning and also credentialing."

## Use Cases

### Learner

- **Personalized AI tutoring sessions**
- **Personalized learning paths**

### Teacher

- **Classroom teacher**: “How are my students doing in Lesson 7?”
- **MOOC**: “Describe the dropoff funnel for my course. I need to know where students are disengaging and what’s causing them to quit.”

### Employer

- **Interactive resume**: Instead of claiming “JavaScript” as a skill on a static resume, humans can link to verified and queryable corpuses. The employer can ask, “How well does this candidate actually understand JavaScript?” and receive a detailed response that cites actual evidence.

### Parent

- **Interactive report card**: Instead of reading a report card that says, “Your child is meeting expectations in reading,” a parent could actually understand the details of what has been taught and learned.

## Why is this new in 2026?

LLMs are superhuman at:

- Producing detailed evidence of learning based on human interaction with software.
- Organizing, summarizing, and interconnecting a lot of information.
- Fetching informations that's spread across many files and folders.

Recent big improvements in LLMs and their orchestration are critical.
